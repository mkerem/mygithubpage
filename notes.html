"Hooked" by Nir Eyal

- Hook Model: Trigger -> Action -> Variable Reward -> Investment - A habit is a behavior done with little or no conscious thought. They are LIFO (Last In, First Out), in other words, habits engrained from the beginning are more likely to stick with us. - John Gourville: "Many innovations fail because consumers irrationally overvalue the old, while companies irrationally overvalue the new." - Products that require a high degree of behavior change are doomed to fail even if benefits of using the product are clear and substantial. - Determine habit-forming products with 2 facotrs: 1) Frequency, and 2) Perceived utility. - Is your idea a vitamin or a painkiller? A painkiller solves an obvious need, relieves a specific pain, often has quantifiable market. - Habit-forming technologies are both: at first nice to have vitamins, but once the habit is established, it becomes an ongoing pain remedy. - For ex. Twitter started off as a vitamin, but turned into a painkiller; we need it to solve our craving for what's going on. - New definition: A habit is when not doing an action causes a bit of pain. - Though make sure you're building healthy habits, not unhealthy addictions. - Toyota method: The 5 Why's: By repeating "Why" five times, we can get closer to the solution. - B = MAT, where B = behavior; M = motivation; A = ability; T= trigger - Any technology or product that significantly reduces the steps to complete a task will enjoy high adoption rates by the people it assists. - 6 elements of simplicity: time, money, physical effort, brain cycles, social deviance, non-routine.
